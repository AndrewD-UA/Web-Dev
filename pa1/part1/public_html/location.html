Indianapolis, Indiana

Background

Indianapolis, Indiana is the home of the Indianapolis Colts and the capital of Indiana. Also known as "The Crossroads of America," Indianapolis sits at the intersection of a large number of interstate highways. As of 2020, Indianapolis had a population of 887,642, and covered a total of 367.93 square miles. Indianapolis was founded in 1820, but did not begin growing in a significant manner until the Civil War, where Indianapolis served as a major logistics hub. Indianapolis falls in the Eastern Time Zone. Most interestingly, Indianapolis's downtown area is formed like a typical grid, but with an X running through the grid to facilitate traffic directly to the circle at the center of Indianapolis containing the Soldiers and Sailor's Monument.

Notable Locations

  • Gainbridge Fieldhouse, formerly Bankers Life Fieldhouse
  • Lucas Oil Stadium
    • Lucas Oil Stadium, official stadium of the Colts, also contains numerous monuments to excellent current and former players, such as Peyton Manning and Jonathan Taylor
  • Soldiers and Sailors Monument
  • Indianapolis Motor Speedway (IMS), home of the Indy 500 NASCAR race
