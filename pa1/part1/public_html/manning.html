Peyton Manning

Background

One of the best Colts players, and players in the broader NFL, Peyton Manning consistently let the Colts to the playoffs. First drafted by the Colts with the first pick of the 1998 draft as a quarterback, Manning intially struggled on the field. His first season, Manning set a new league record for most interceptions (28). However, as the Colts began to build around Manning, his talent as a pocket passer began to show through.

Colts Tenure

Throughout Manning's stint with the Colts from the 1999-2010 seasons, the Colts only missed the playoffs a single time in 2001. In 2010, Peyton Manning suffered a catastrophic neck injury that led to his abstaining from the 2011 season. This led to a catastrophic performance in the 2011 season, and the Colts drafted Andrew Luck following the season as a new quarterback. The Colts simultaneously parted ways with Peyton Manning, who went to the Denver Broncos for another four seasons and another Super Bowl victory. Manning's split with the Colts is oft referenced during the current spat between Colts owner Jim Irsay and running back Jonathan Taylor.

Statistics

As of his retirement after the 2015 seasons, Peyton Manning has the following statistics:

  • 539 passing touchdowns
  • 71,940 passing yards
  • 14 pro bowl appearances
  • 4 Super Bowl appearances
    • Indianapolis Colts: 2006, 2009
    • Denver Broncos: 2013, 2015
  • 18 total seasons, 17 of which were played and 15 of which included playoff appearances
  • 186-69 record
